Artichoke

Complete Guide On How To Grow Artichoke

Set out artichoke crowns or root divisions in spring about 2 weeks before the last frost.

The artichoke is an edible thistle flower bud that is eaten before it opens. Artichokes are tender perennials that grow from 3 to 4 feet (.9-1.2m) tall and to 6 feet 1.8m) across.

How to Grow Artichoke

When to Plant Artichoke

  1. Plant artichokes on the average date of the last frost in late winter or early spring.
  2. Set your root divisions up to 2 weeks before the last frost.
  3. The optimal planting soil temperature is between 50° and 85°F (10-29.4°C).

Where to Plant Artichoke

  1. Artichokes grow best where there are mild, frost-free winters and long, moist summers.
  2. (Artichokes are commonly grown along the central California coast and along the southern Atlantic and Gulf coasts.)
  3. The optimal growing temperature is not less than 50°F (10°C) at night and not more than 75°F (24°C) during the day.
  4. Do not plant artichokes where there are fewer than 100 frost-free growing days.

How to Plant Artichoke

  1. Grow artichokes from offshoots, suckers, or seed. Plant artichokes in full sun.
  2. Artichokes require rich, well-drained, moisture-retentive soil with a soil pH between 6.0 and 6.8.
  3. Add compost and well-aged manure to planting beds in advance of planting.

Water Requirements for Artichoke

  1. Keep the soil evenly moist.
  2. Allow the soil to dry between watering.
  3. Add a low nitrogen fertilizer such as 5-10-10 to planting beds in spring and fall.

Protecting Against Disease and Pests

  1. Aphids and plume moths attack the artichoke.
  2. The plume moth is a problem in heavy artichoke-growing districts. Hose off aphids.
  3. Crown rot may infect plants covered in winter. Do not place mulch until the soil temperature drops to 40°F (4.4C).
  4. Remove mulch as soon as the weather begins to warm.
  5. Plant disease-resistant varieties when they are available.
  6. Avoid handling plants when they are wet.
  7. Remove infected plants and destroy them.

How to Harvest Artichoke

  1. Artichokes are harvested beginning the second year.
  2. Cut buds about the size of an apple before they open.
  3. Cut stems 1½ inches (3.8cm) below the bud.

Storing Artichoke

  1. Artichokes will keep in the refrigerator for up to one week.
  2. Artichokes hearts can be frozen after cooking, canned, or pickled.

Different Types of Artichoke

  1. Green Globe.
  2. Imperial Star.
  3. Violetto.
