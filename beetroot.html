Beetroot

Complete Guide On How To Grow Beetroot

Beets are a cool-weather crop.Beets are biennial plants grown as annuals.Plant 5 to 10 beets per household member.

They are grown for their swollen roots and also for their leaves. There are two main beet types: globe-shaped and long-rooted. Beetroots can be red, orange, gold, yellow, white, and even concentrically ringed roots. A rosette of large edible leaves sprouts from the root; green leaves can have red, yellow, or white veins.

How to Grow Beetroot

When to Plant Beetroot

  1. Sow beets in the garden 2 to 3 weeks before the last average frost date in spring.
  2. The soil temperature should be at least 50°F (10°C), especially if you have experienced damping-off.
  3. Beets will grow faster in spring when covered with a row cover which will provide protection from chilling temperatures and wind.
  4. Beets require 45 to 65 days to reach harvest.
  5. Beets can again be planted in late summer or early autumn 6 to 8 weeks before the first average frost in autumn.
  6. This will be the fall and winter storage crop, so sow only once, planting as much as you will need for winter.
  7. When direct sowing during summer, keep the soil constantly moist or germination will be poor.

Where to Plant Beetroot

  1. Grow beets in full sun or partial shade in warm regions. Plant beets in well-worked loose soil rich in organic matter.
  2. Be sure to remove all stones and clods from planting beds so as not to impede or split growing roots.
  3. Add plenty of aged compost to growing beds in advance of planting, this will increase the yield.
  4. Alternatively, add 3 cups (700ml) of dried seaweed per 100 square feet (9 sq m).
  5. Beets grow best where the soil pH is 6.0 to 6.8; the ideal soil pH is 6.5.
  6. Add only aged manure to the soil, fresh manures are high in nitrogen and cause beetroots to fork, twist, or be “hairy.”

How to Plant Beetroot

  1. Beets are grown from seed clusters about the size of a small pea. Each cluster contains several tiny true seeds.
  2. Presoak seed clusters for 12 hours before sowing to help speed germination.
  3. Sow seed clusters 1 inch (2.5cm) deep and 1 inch apart.
  4. Seedlings will sprout in clumps.
  5. Use small scissors to thin successful seedlings to 4 to 6 inches (10-15cm) apart when seedlings are 3 inches tall.
  6. Alternatively, you can gently separate young seedlings and replant the extra seedlings in a row nearby.
  7. Space rows 12 to 18 inches (30-45cm) apart. Add thinned seedlings to salads. Beets generally do not transplant well.

Water Requirements for Beetroot

  1. Keep beets evenly watered.
  2. Do not let the soil dry out.
  3. Lack of water will cause roots to become stunted, stringy, and tough.
  4. Add aged compost to planting beds in advance of seeding.
  5. Side dress beets with compost at midseason.

Protecting Against Disease and Pests

  1. Check roots for boring insects.
  2. Leafminers can tunnel inside the leaf surface leaving gray streaks.
  3. Alternaria leaf spot is caused by various species of fungi in the Alternaria genus.
  4. If your plant has it, you’ll notice the leaves crinkling and curling inward.
  5. They may also be stunted, small, and discolored, with purple margins.

How to Harvest Beetroot

  1. Beets will reach harvestable size–1 to 3 inches (2.5-7.6cm) in diameter–40 to 80 days after sowing.
  2. Lift beets gently. Twist the leaves off rather than cutting them off to prevent juices from bleeding.
  3. Baby beets are ready for harvest about 40 days after planting.
  4. Full-size beets will be ready about 2 to 3 weeks later.
  5. Roots about the size of a golf ball will have optimal flavor.
  6. ‘Lutz Greenleaf’ variety will be flavorful when as large as a softball.
  7. Golden beets will be flavorful when the size of a baseball.

Storing Beetroot

  1. Beets will keep in the refrigerator for 1 to 3 weeks.
  2. Beet greens will keep in the refrigerator in a plastic bag for up to 1 week.
  3. Beets will keep for 1 to 3 months in damp sawdust in a cold, moist place.
  4. Beets can be frozen and dried.
  5. For long-term storage, you can pack beets in containers of moist sand or peat and keep them in an unheated basement or garage (40-50°F/4.5-10°C is ideal).
  6. beets will not store as long as larger beets.
  7. In cold-winter regions, you can store beets in an outdoor pit lined with dry leaves and straw.

Different Types of Beetroot

  1. ‘Burpee’s Golden’ has sweet golden flesh which is nonstaining, the greens can be used for cooking or salads, grow from seed in 55 days.
  2. ‘Chioggia’ has sweet roots with rings of red and white when sliced, use the greens in place of spinach, grow from seed in 54 days.
  3. ‘Cylindra’ has long dark red roots which are ideal for pickling, ready for harvest 60 days from seed.
  4. ‘Lutz Green Leaf’ is a good choice for winter storage, it has a dark red root and tasty greens, ready 70 to 80 days from seed sowing.
  5. ‘Red Ace’ is fast growing with sweet roots and tasty greens, it is resistant to leaf spot, ready 50 days from seed.
