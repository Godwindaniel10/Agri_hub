Fennel

Complete Guide On How To Grow Fennel

Florence fennel or finocchio is grown primarily for its bulbous base and leaf stalks which are used as vegetables.

Fennel is a stocky plant that can grow to 24 inches (61cm) tall and looks something like celery with fleshy stalks and feathery leaves. A taller cultivar of fennel known as common or sweet fennel is grown for its leaves and seeds which are used as herbal seasonings. Fennel produces a flat-topped cluster of small, golden flowers. Both Florence fennel and common fennel are members of the parsley family.

How to Grow Fennel

When to Plant Fennel

  1. Florence fennel is a cool-weather perennial grown as an annual.
  2. Fennel can be sown in the garden as early as 2 to 3 weeks before the average last frost date in spring.
  3. Seeds germinate best at 60°F (17°C).
  4. Fennel will tolerate heat and cold but does best when it comes to maturity in cool weather.
  5. Fennel requires 90 to 115 frost-free days to reach harvest.
  6. For an autumn crop sow fennel in mid- to late summer.

Where to Plant Fennel

  1. Grow fennel in full sun.
  2. Plant fennel in well-drained soil rich in organic matter.
  3. Prepare the bed in advance by working in aged compost.
  4. Fennel prefers a soil pH of 5.5 to 6.8.

How to Plant Fennel

  1. Sow fennel seed a ¼ inch deep 4 to 6 inches (10-15cm) apart.
  2. Thin successful seedlings to 12 inches (30cm) apart.
  3. Space rows 24 to 36 inches (61-91cm) apart.

Water Requirements for Fennel

  1. Keep fennel on the dry side
  2. The soil should be evenly moist but not wet.
  3. Mulch to retain soil moisture in hot regions.
  4. Prepare planting beds with aged compost.
  5. Side dress fennel with aged compost at midseason.

Protecting Against Disease and Pests

  1. Fennel may be attacked by the parsley caterpillar.
  2. Remove it by hand.
  3. Fennel has no serious disease problems.

How to Harvest Fennel

  1. Fennel’s bulbous stalk can be harvested when it is 3 inches (7cm) or more in diameter.
  2. Cut the whole stalk like celery just below the point where individual stalks join together.
  3. Cut leaves as needed once they have reached 18 inches (45cm) tall.
  4. Florence fennel will be ready for harvest 90 to 115 days after sowing.
  5. Common fennel sprigs can be cut for flavoring once the plant is established.

Storing Fennel

  1. Fennel is best eaten fresh.
  2. Florence fennel will keep in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or in a cold moist place for 2 to 3 months.
  3. Stalks can be frozen or dried.Fennel leaves can be frozen or dried as herbs; dried leaves should be kept in an airtight container.
  4. Fennel leaves can be frozen or dried as herbs; dried leaves should be kept in an airtight container.

Growing Different Types of Fennel

  1. ‘Hearld’ for spring planting.
  2. ‘Zefa Fino’
  3. ‘Rudy’
  4. ‘Trieste’ have large, flavorful bulbs.
