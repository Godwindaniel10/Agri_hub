Chokos

Complete Guide On How To Grow Chokos

Chokos is a warm-season, tender perennial. Plant the whole fruit 3 to 4 weeks after the last average frost date in spring when the weather has warmed. Chokos is alos knows as chokos.

Chokos is a vine that produces a pale green to white, flattened-pear-shaped fruit that tastes like a nutty-flavored squash. Vine-like stems grow from a tuberous root and can reach up to 50 feet (15.2m) long. Leaves are hairy and resemble maple-leaves; male and female flowers are borne on the same vine. Young shoots, the fruit, and mature tubers are edible.

How to Grow Chokos

When to Plant Chokos

  1. Plant chokos 3 to 4 weeks after the last average frost date in spring when the soil temperature has reached at least 65°F (18°C).
  2. Chokos grows best where summer temperatures are warm to hot, in tropical or subtropical regions such as Florida, the Gulf Coast, and California.
  3. Chokos requires 120 to 150 frost-free warm days to reach harvest.
  4. In short-summer regions, grow chokos in a container so that it can be brought indoors when the temperatures cool.

Where to Plant Chokos

  1. Plant chokos in full sun; chokos will grow in partial shade but the yield will be reduced.
  2. Grow chokos in loose, well-drained but moisture-retentive soil rich in organic matter.
  3. Chokos prefers a soil pH of 6.0 to 6.8.

How to Plant Chokos

  1. Set a whole chokos fruit about 4 to 6 inches (10-15cm)deep, fat end down, and at an angle so that the stem end is just level with the soil surface.
  2. Sow seeds or fruits 10 feet apart. Chokos is a vigorous climber; set a sturdy trellis or support in place at planting.
  3. Do not allow maturing fruit to come in contact with the soil; it will spoil and germinate while still attached to the vine.

Water Requirements for Chokos

  1. Give chokos even, regular water; do not let the soil dry out. Add aged compost to the planting bed before planting.
  2. Side dress chokos with compost tea every 4 to 6 weeks during the growing season.
  3. Side dress chokos with aged compost at midseason.
  4. Put a trellis or stake supports in place at planting time.

Protecting Against Disease and Pests

  1. Aphids may attack chokos vines.
  2. Hand-pick or hose them off with a strong blast of water.
  3. Chokos has no serious disease problems.

How to Harvest Chokos

  1. Chokos will be ready for harvest when the fruit is tender and about 4 to 6 inches (10-15cm) in diameter, usually 120 to 150 warm, frost-free days after planting.
  2. Cut chokos from the vine with a knife or hand-pruner. Harvest chokos before the flesh gets hard.
